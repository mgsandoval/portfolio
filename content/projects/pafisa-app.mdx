## The problem

{/* What was broken or missing before this dashboard existed? */}

Pafisa is a manufacturer of toilet paper and napkins; the company had no way to measure the quality of its products.

Pafisa also lacked control over its pallets of toilet paper and napkins, as well as a user-friendly interface where they could
easily transfer inventory in SAP Business One.

Product tracking was done using Microsoft Excel spreadsheets, and data had to be entered manually, which resulted in 
errors on the part of the operators.


## What I built

{/* Your role, the architecture, the trickiest part of wiring into SAP B1. */}

I have built a web-based system where users can enter each of the parameters for the products manufactured by Pafisa.
The operator enters the parameters for each product, and the system automatically calculates the quality percentage of the entered product;
a photograph of the product is taken and saved.

The system also allows for better control over the company’s product pallets, with a user-friendly interface where
stock transfers can be easily made in SAP B1 and, above all, the entered data can be kept organized.

The system includes history reports by area, audits by area, user roles, user management, and user permissions.


## Outcome

{/* Impact: time saved, errors reduced, teams using it, etc. */}

Operator errors have been reduced, product entry times have been shortened, and operators are
using a system they find very easy to use, even if they don’t have much technical experience.

Information for each product is stored in a database, and users with the appropriate permissions can 
view or modify it, ensuring that only those granted permissions by administrators can manipulate
the information.

Stock transfers to SAP Business One are easily entered, much more so than in the ERP software.

- Reduced manual errors
- Reduced entry time per product
- Simple and user-friendly system
- Reliable system for administrators
- SAP B1 stock transfers are much easier to enter
